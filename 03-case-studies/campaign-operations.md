# Operating Case · Running a Campaign Across Roughly 1,200 Partners

## One-line summary

I turned a complex holiday campaign into a clear daily routine that helped our team move from last place to #1 among five teams in both claim rate and first-trade rate.

## 1. The real growth job

OKX's 2025 Lunar New Year campaign combined three overlapping tracks: red-envelope claims, first-trade tasks and a Lantern Festival prize.

The campaign covered roughly **1,200 partners**. During a seven-day holiday sprint, partners needed clear actions, BD staff needed daily priorities, and reward-farming risk had to be controlled without blocking legitimate acquisition.

## 2. Why the manual approach stopped scaling

The campaign rules existed, but the team lacked a clear way to execute them every day.

- partners did not know which action mattered today;
- BD follow-up varied by person and memory;
- zero-claim partners were not separated from active partners;
- reward budget could be wasted on low-quality or abusive traffic;
- suspected farming could also create false positives that required judgment.

## 3. Before → After

I replaced three overlapping rule sets with one action per day and a short script the team could use immediately. I tracked claims and first trades daily, prioritized zero-claim and high-potential partners, and allocated rewards using ROI and acquisition quality rather than availability alone. When activity looked suspicious, I reviewed the partner's history and user quality before deciding whether to stop support or request another allocation.

## 5. Result

- Our team moved from last place to **#1 among five teams** in both **claim rate and first-trade rate**.
- First-trade rate reached **16–17%**, compared with **5–6%** for the other teams.

The campaign covered roughly 1,200 partners team-wide.

## 6. What broke and where the case stops

- Some communities attempted to farm rewards. Those partners could not be treated as healthy acquisition simply because claims increased.
- Fraud controls could mis-flag a legitimate high-quality partner when a reward link was copied by abusive users. I used historical profitability, acquisition quality and partner behavior to decide when to provide a human guarantee and request a second allocation.
- AI-generated scripts reduced cognitive load, but they did not decide who deserved budget or who had been misclassified.

## 7. My role versus AI

| I owned | AI supported |
|---|---|
| Diagnosed an execution-system gap rather than a campaign-design gap | Turned complex rules into concise daily scripts and timeline reminders |
| Set prioritization and reward-allocation logic | Reduced repetitive content preparation |
| Read daily data and chose interventions | Helped structure recurring updates |
| Reviewed abuse risk and argued for legitimate partners | Did not make final budget, fraud or relationship decisions |
