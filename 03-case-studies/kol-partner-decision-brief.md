# KOL Partner Decision Brief · Candidate R-07

> **Sanitized decision brief based on the real research workflow.**

**Status:** decision-ready brief based on one real candidate, not a composite. Identifying details have been removed and the product context generalized. Outreach and partnership outcome have not yet been tested.

## Product context

**Sanitized brief:** a research-led cross-market product for Chinese-speaking users who already follow AI, digital assets and public markets. The partnership should build trust through useful education, not price promises or a generic affiliate placement.

## Candidate profile

- Independent Chinese-language research creator.
- Covers the intersection of AI, digital assets and public equities.
- Uses explanatory posts to connect product mechanics with user implications.
- Has published both research-style breakdowns and product-related content.

## Audience / product fit

**Directional fit: medium-high.** The candidate can plausibly bridge a product from crypto-native users to a wider technology-and-finance audience. That is useful for an educational offer, but topic overlap alone does not prove commercial intent or conversion quality.

## Evidence for

| Evidence observed in the real research workflow | Partnership implication |
|---|---|
| Repeated cross-market analysis rather than a single topical mention | The audience is likely accustomed to moving between AI, public markets and digital assets. |
| Explanatory content that turns product or market mechanics into user-facing implications | A hands-on educational format fits better than a generic promotional post. |
| Evidence of testing or discussing financial and AI products | A real product walkthrough is plausible if the creator is willing to use the product first. |
| A deliberately maintained creator positioning rather than an empty or purely referral-led profile | The account merits discovery time instead of automatic rejection at screening. |

## Evidence against

| Evidence gap or risk | Decision implication |
|---|---|
| The content spans several markets | Topic overlap may be broad curiosity rather than strong intent for this product. |
| Product-related posts coexist with independent analysis | Editorial independence and sponsorship fit need to be tested, not assumed. |
| The archive does not contain first-party audience geography, user composition or conversion history | Reach cannot be used as a proxy for qualified acquisition. |
| The evidence is strong for one research month, not a longitudinal partnership record | The judgment should remain provisional until current behavior is checked. |

## A/B/C/D priority

**Priority: B — run one focused discovery call and pilot; do not scale yet.**

| Priority | Meaning |
|---|---|
| A | Pursue now with a tailored offer; fit and evidence are already strong. |
| B | Run a focused discovery call or pilot; fit is plausible but one important question remains. |
| C | Monitor; the signal is interesting but not strong enough to spend active acquisition time. |
| D | Do not pursue under the current product brief. |

Candidate R-07 is B because the content bridge is real, while audience quality and product intent are still unverified.

## Recommended offer

Propose one co-created, hands-on explainer: the candidate tests the product, shows the cross-market use case and states who it is—and is not—for. Keep the first collaboration educational and measurable. Do not lead with a generic referral code, price call or long-term package.

## First outreach action

Send a short, tailored note that references the candidate's recurring cross-market teaching pattern without quoting a private research record:

> Your strongest work connects AI, public-market and digital-asset mechanics for the same curious finance audience. I would like to test one hands-on educational collaboration rather than a generic placement. Before discussing scale, could we spend 20 minutes checking audience fit, product use and one qualified success event?

Before approving a pilot, request first-party evidence for audience geography and topic mix, confirm that the creator will use the product, and agree on the qualified action that the test should produce.

## What would change the decision

- **Upgrade B → A:** first-party audience evidence confirms the target-user overlap; the creator uses the product and can explain a genuine use case; a small pilot produces the agreed qualified action against its baseline.
- **Move B → C:** the content fit remains credible, but audience intent or willingness to test the product stays unclear.
- **Move B → D:** the audience is materially outside the target group, the creator will only accept generic exposure without using the product, or the proposed content creates a compliance or trust mismatch.

## Stop condition

Stop before the pilot if audience evidence or genuine product use cannot be established. If a pilot runs, stop after that single test when the pre-agreed qualified action is not met and the result reveals no fixable message or onboarding issue. Do not continue because reach or engagement looks large in isolation.
