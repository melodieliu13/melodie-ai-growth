# Reconstructed Example · Partner Opportunity Decision

> **Reconstructed and sanitized example based on the original operating workflow.**
>
> This is not an original OKX file, historical product screen or employer record. Names, identifying details and confidential commercial terms have been removed.

## Input

**Prospect:** a regional quantitative-trading community outside the team's usual crypto-affiliate circle.

**Public and relationship signals available at the time:**

- an existing audience interested in systematic trading rather than token promotion;
- evidence of active offline community relationships;
- users already comparing multiple exchanges;
- a high learning barrier around exchange onboarding and product differences;
- no evidence yet that the prospect would move meaningful activity.

## Decision output

| Decision layer | Operating judgment |
|---|---|
| Opportunity | Adjacent-circle prospect: potentially valuable because the community already understands trading, but not yet proven as an acquisition channel. |
| Fit | Strong product fit for quantitative-trading users; weaker fit for a generic “open an exchange account” pitch. |
| Priority | **A for deep research and a tailored meeting**, not automatic onboarding. |
| Offer angle | Lead with product capability, onboarding support and a clear competitor comparison; do not lead with a generic rebate promise. |
| First action | Prepare a compact partner brief, invite the prospect into a low-pressure offline setting, then test whether they will nominate a first user cohort. |
| Evidence required to advance | Named user cohort, willingness to complete onboarding, and early trading activity—not enthusiasm alone. |
| Stop condition | No identifiable users, repeated interest without action, or a request that creates compliance or quality risk. |

## Before → After

**Before:** “This community looks relevant; send the standard partnership deck.”

**After:** “The audience-product fit is plausible. Test it with a tailored product conversation and a small onboarding cohort before allocating more resources.”

## Boundary

This example demonstrates the decision structure used in the expansion workflow. It does not claim that the reconstructed prospect is the specific partner that later reached 200M USDT in monthly trading volume. That portfolio milestone was reached after my departure, and this reconstruction does not reproduce any original internal record.
