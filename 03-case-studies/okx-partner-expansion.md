# Operating Case · Finding New Partner Opportunities at OKX

## One-line summary

I combined AI-assisted prospect research with offline relationship building to expand beyond familiar crypto circles and open new partner channels.

## 1. The real growth job

The job was not “build a prospecting tool.” It was to find partner opportunities in a regional market where repeated outreach to the same crypto-native circles had started to plateau.

Promising partners were dispersed across quantitative trading, FinTech, cross-border commerce and local business communities. A name on a list was not enough: I needed to understand the prospect's resources, likely needs, fit with OKX and the offer that could make a conversation worth having.

## 2. Why the manual approach stopped scaling

The old workflow depended on personal memory, fragmented online searches and generic outreach. It created three problems:

- adjacent communities were easy to miss;
- background research was repeated for every conversation;
- generic product pitches did not explain why a specific partner should care.

Pure online outreach also underperformed because high-value partnerships required trust, context and repeated human contact.

## 3. Before → After

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| Search inside familiar crypto circles | Search adjacent FinTech, quant and cross-border networks |
| Keep prospect context across scattered notes | Use AI-assisted research to build a compact partner brief |
| Send a generic exchange proposition | Match partner resources and needs to a relevant offer |
| Explain onboarding repeatedly | Prepare reusable onboarding and competitor-comparison materials |
| Treat online outreach as the whole funnel | Use research to prepare low-pressure offline meetings and follow-up |

## 5. Result

- I personally onboarded **5 new cross-sector affiliate partners**.
- The team onboarded about **15**.
- One quantitative-trading partner developed through the team initiative later reached **200M USDT in monthly trading volume** and qualified for the highest partner tier. This milestone was reached after I had left OKX.

## 6. What broke and where the case stops

- Better research did not replace relationship work. The useful step was turning research into a reason to meet, then building trust offline.
- A technically plausible partner was not automatically a priority. Resource fit, motivation and the ability to onboard users still required human judgment.
- The original internal product evidence is unavailable, so this is an operating case study rather than reproducible software evidence.
- The **200M USDT** result belongs to a team-developed partnership and was reached after my departure. It is not presented as a result achieved during my tenure or attributed to one person or an AI tool alone.

## 7. My role versus AI

| I owned | AI supported |
|---|---|
| Reframed plateauing growth as a circle-expansion problem | Accelerated public-web prospect discovery and background research |
| Judged partner fit, motivation and relationship priority | Helped structure partner briefs and comparison material |
| Designed the offer and onboarding path | Reduced repetitive preparation work |
| Built trust through meetings and follow-up | Did not own the relationship or business decision |
