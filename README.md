# I use AI to find growth opportunities, decide which partners to pursue, and execute at scale.

I'm Melodie Liu, a global growth operator with eight years of experience across education, FinTech and Web3. I use AI when research or execution stops scaling. It helps me find and process information; I still decide what to do.

**The work:** Find opportunities → Make decisions → Execute at scale

## Career track record

- Managed a portfolio of roughly 300 affiliate partners that generated 25M+ USDT in cumulative profit at OKX.
- Increased monthly first trades by approximately **454%** and rebates by **65%** in one KOL partnership case.
- Worked across global communities and partner ecosystems in English and Chinese, including an English ZK Sync Space that helped lift weekly active bridge addresses **8%**.

## 1 · Find opportunities — Affiliate Partner Acquisition at OKX

Regional growth had plateaued inside the same crypto circles. I reframed the problem as a partner-discovery challenge: find adjacent FinTech, quantitative-trading and cross-border-commerce communities, understand their fit, and prepare a reason for each prospect to engage.

**Before → After:** ad hoc online prospecting → AI-assisted prospect discovery and background research → partner-fit judgment → tailored onboarding and competitor comparison → offline relationship building.

**Result:** I personally onboarded **5 cross-sector affiliate partners**; the team onboarded about **15**. One quantitative-trading partner developed through the team initiative later reached **200M USDT in monthly trading volume**.

## 2 · Make decisions — KOL Partner Intelligence

KOL research used to mean opening profiles one by one, reading timelines and trying to remember who looked promising. That worked for ten accounts, not hundreds.

I built a Chrome extension and a search tool that made **197 accounts and 11,100 posts** queryable. The tool finds the relevant material; I decide whether it changes who to pursue or what to test.

**Before → After:** repeated timeline reading → searchable evidence → source comparison → a clear recommendation and next action.

**My decision:** **B — run one focused discovery call and pilot; do not scale yet.** The brief records the evidence for and against, the offer I would make, the first outreach, the conditions for upgrading or downgrading the candidate, and when I would stop.

## 3 · Execute at scale — 1,200-partner Campaign Operations

During OKX's 2025 Lunar New Year campaign, three activity tracks were running at the same time across roughly **1,200 partners**. Partners did not know which action mattered that day, holiday follow-up depended too much on individual memory, and a higher claim count could still mean reward farming rather than healthy acquisition. I reduced the campaign to one daily action, used claim and first-trade data to decide who needed follow-up, allocated rewards by ROI and partner quality, and kept suspected abuse under human review.

**Result:** our team moved from last place to **#1 among five teams** in both claim rate and first-trade rate. First-trade rate reached **16–17%**, compared with **5–6%** for the other teams.
